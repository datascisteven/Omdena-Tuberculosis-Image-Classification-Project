Tuberculosis (TB) prevention is crucial to reduce the spread of this contagious lung infection. Key preventive measures include:

  1. Avoid Close Contact: Steer clear of close contact with individuals who have active TB disease to minimize the risk of infection.
  2. Treat Latent TB: If diagnosed with latent TB infection, consider taking medicine to prevent the progression to TB disease.
  3. Vaccination: In some countries, the BCG vaccine helps prevent severe forms of TB in children.
  4. Promote Immune Health: Maintaining a healthy immune system through a balanced diet, regular exercise, and proper sleep can reduce susceptibility.
  5. Early Diagnosis and Treatment: Rapidly diagnosing and treating active TB can prevent its transmission.
  6. Isolation and Quarantine: Isolate individuals with active TB, and implement quarantine measures when necessary.
  7. Infection Control: Implement administrative and environmental procedures in healthcare settings to reduce exposure to TB.
  8. Health Hygiene: Practicing good respiratory hygiene, like covering your mouth and nose when coughing or sneezing, helps prevent the spread.
